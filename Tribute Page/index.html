Service Before Self

Indian Heroes

Vikram Batra

(9 September 1974 – 7 July 1999)

Vikram Batra was an Indian Army Officer. He was awarded the Param Vir Chakra, the highest indian military decoration, for his actions during the kargil war, On 7th July 1999, Vikram was killed while fighting pakistani troops in kargil district of Jammu Kashmir. He was the hero of kargil war in 1999.

Sandeep Unnikrishnan

(15 March 1977 – 28 November 2008)

Sandeep Unnikrishnan, was an Indian Army officer, who was serving in the 51 Special Action Group of the National Security Guards on deputation. He was killed in action during the November 2008 Mumbai attacks. He was consequently awarded the Ashoka Chakra, India's highest peacetime gallantry award, on 26 January 2009

We Salute Our Bravehearts

Indian Army upholds the security of our nation and it is all because of our defense army that we constantly feel a sense of security and are able to live and breathe freely, without any sense of fear. The supreme sacrifice of soldiers in the line of duty, the sacrifice of their family members, and future challenges in changing global perspective highlighting that the Indian Army is always more than ready to protect its borders., I salute the tradition of courage and valour of the Indian Army. I remember the bravery of those courageous soldiers. "Their sacrifice is a source of inspiration for all Indians. This country will always remember them. India will always remain indebted to soldiers for their supreme sacrifice". Since-Independence, more than 26,000 soldiers of the Indian Armed Forces have made the supreme sacrifice to defend the sovereignty and integrity of the country. We should neither underestimate nor fail to remember the role of Indian army in upholding the security of the nation. Keeping in mind the extremely important role our Indian army plays in ensuring the safety of our country.
